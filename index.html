Formula One began in 1950 with the first official World Championship race at Silverstone in the United Kingdom, although its roots go back further to European Grand Prix racing in the 1920s and 1930s. Early competitions were shaped by legendary manufacturers like Ferrari, Alfa Romeo, and Maserati, with drivers competing in dangerous conditions where safety was minimal. Over the decades, the sport evolved significantly, introducing innovations such as aerodynamic design, advanced materials, and stricter safety regulations following several tragic accidents. By the late 20th century, Formula One had become a global spectacle, featuring iconic drivers like Ayrton Senna and Michael Schumacher. Today, Formula One is a highly sophisticated and technologically advanced sport, with hybrid power units, data-driven strategies, and worldwide audiences. Modern stars such as Lewis Hamilton and Max Verstappen continue to push the limits, while the sport expands into new markets and focuses more on sustainability and entertainment, making it more popular than ever.
